Assessing Organic Carbon Variability in Missouri: A study on soil organic carbon using spatial statistics and machine learning

Soil organic carbon (OC) plays a critical role in the global carbon cycle and climate regulation, but human activities such as agriculture and land-use changes are altering its distribution. This study explores the spatial variability of OC in Missouri, a key region for carbon sequestration and greenhouse gas emissions, by integrating machine learning and spatial statistics. Using random forest modeling in ArcGIS Pro alongside Geographically Weighted Regression (GWR), this project identifies key soil, topographic, and vegetation factors influencing OC distribution.

Mapping Mangrove Diversity: Hyperspectral Classification on Lothian Island

Mangroves are vital ecosystems that contribute to coastal resilience and biodiversity. However, with approximately 35% of global mangrove forests degrading over the past two decades, effective monitoring and classification of these ecosystems are crucial for conservation efforts. This project explores the use of hyperspectral imaging to classify and assess mangrove species on Lothian Island, India. By analyzing spectral data from AVIRIS-NG and a self-compiled spectral library, we applied advanced classification techniques in ENVI, to identify different mangrove species.

Predictive Modelling with Advanced Statistics

This project explores machine learning techniques to predict per capita crime rates and median home values in Boston using regression models, random forests, and support vector machines (SVMs) using R-Programming. This study highlights how different machine learning models can be fine-tuned for urban analytics, balancing accuracy, interpretability, and predictive power.

Suitability model for tree planting priority

Urbanization and climate change have increased environmental challenges, making urban green spaces essential for resilience and well-being. This study develops a suitability model to prioritize tree planting in Los Angeles using data from Esri’s Shade Equity: Determine Tree Planting Locations with Suitability Analysis course and social vulnerability data from the County of Los Angeles. The model evaluates five equally weighted factors—canopy cover, traffic density, land surface temperature, social vulnerability, and air pollution—to identify census tracts that would benefit most from additional tree planting.
