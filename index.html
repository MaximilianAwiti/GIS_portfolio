About Me

My GIS and remote sensing journey began with a background in planning, where I quickly saw the power of data-driven decision-making. A single course sparked my curiosity, and my passion grew as I explored how spatial data can solve real-world problems. Since then, I’ve developed my skills through ESRI courses and hands-on projects, constantly finding new ways to apply geospatial analysis.

What intrigues me most about GIS and remote sensing is the blend of problem-solving, design, and real-world impact. Whether analyzing environmental challenges, conducting suitability studies, or planning sustainable communities, GIS bridges raw data and actionable insights that shape better decisions.

I aim to use GIS to drive positive change in urban planning, clean energy, and climate resilience. I believe geospatial analysis holds the key to tackling some of today’s most pressing challenges.
